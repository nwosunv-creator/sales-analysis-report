Story of the data and Methodology

The data for the Koma Flamy Sales Report was collected from the companyâ€™s internal sales records, capturing key variables such as product category, region, customer demographics, quantity sold, cost, and profit. The dataset was first cleaned in Microsoft Excel to remove duplicates, correct inconsistencies, handle missing values and ensure accuracy.

After cleaning, the data was analyzed using Excel Pivot Tables to identify sales trends, high-performing products, and profit variations across different locations and customer segments. Visual dashboards were created to highlight key metrics such as total sales, top-selling products, and regional performance. This analytical approach provided actionable insights to support better decision-making and improve future sales strategies.

post analysis

In the product category of accessories, the state of California recorded the highest purchases. The primary buyers were adults (35-64) and young adults (25-34). At the country level, the United States led in sales, with male customers contributing the majority. The sales trend report highlights 2013 and 2014 as the best-performing years

In the bike category, the state of California recorded the highest sales, with adults (35-64) as the leading buyers, followed by young adults (25-34). At the country level, the United States dominated performance, with both male and female customers contributing significantly. The sales trend report identifies 2015 and 2016 as the strongest years for bike sales.

For the clothing category, sales were strongest in California, with adults (35-64) making the largest contribution. At the country level, the United States accounted for the majority of purchases, with participation from both male and female customers. However, the sales trend recorded a sharp decline in Quarter 3 of 2014 and again in Quarter 3 of 2016, indicating seasonal or market-driven challenges
